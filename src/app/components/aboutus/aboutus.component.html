What is Newton MUN?

Newton Model United Nations is an event at which students from different schools and backgrounds have an opportunity to simulate discussions that take place at the United Nations. By adhering to the organization's rules of procedure, delegates will delve into the intricacies of diplomacy, negotiations and international relations to produce actionable resolutions to delicate topics. Newton Model United Nations 2024 will take place through an in-person format at the Newton College campus on the 20th and 21st of April.

Newton Model United Nations 2024 aims to foster diplomacy, collaboration, and authentic critical thinking. Delegates, bringing diverse backgrounds and expertise, will engage in multilateral discussions covering timely topics, offering valuable insights into various aspects of world affairs while remaining relevant to our local context. Moreover, this year’s conference will be Newton’s largest conference, with a doubled delegate capacity and an expanded number of committees. Join us in shaping the future of international discourse.

Over two days of intense debate, about 400 secondary school students will discuss issues adapted from the United Nations agenda and its regional bodies which lack mainstream attention. Delegates are encouraged to pursue their passions, casting aside fear and stepping into leadership roles for this highly anticipated event. Inviting them to embrace our slogan: DREAM BEYOND, LEAD BEYOND: TRANSFORMING VISIONS INTO REALITY .

The secretariat of Newton MUN 2024

Secretary General Enzo Porto

Enzo Porto, a second-year International Baccalaureate Diploma Programme student at Newton College, aims to pursue Computer Science studies abroad.

Originally from Brazil, he relocated to Peru in 2018. Introduced to the Newton MUN Club by his Spanish Tutor in 2018, Enzo discovered his passion for debate and public speaking, stemming from his involvement in TEDx in 2017. With nearly six years of MUN experience, he has garnered multiple awards and progressed from an Executive Member in 2021 to Secretary-General in 2023.

Outside MUN, Enzo actively participates in STEM activities, including programming and leading the STEM Club at Newton College. His commitment to activism and community service activities led him to be awarded first place in the Sea Beyond Competition and be invited as a guest speaker in the 2022 UN Ocean Conference, in Lisbon. Enzo's dedication led him to establish his NGO, Oceanica, and speak at the UNESCO 42nd General Conference in Paris in 2023.

Through MUN, Enzo honed skills in public speaking, improvisation, and critical thinking, transforming into an ambitious delegate striving for continuous improvement. His MUN journey cultivated a mindset of ambitious risk-taking, evident in his approach to everyday challenges and endeavours.

With this in mind, Enzo sincerely wishes all delegates a remarkable experience at Newton MUN 2024. He encourages all delegates, regardless of experience, to approach the two days of debate with ambition, giving their utmost and fostering a growth mindset.

Under-Secretary General | Director of UNICEF Arantza Cruzado

Arantza Cruzado is a second year student of the IB Diploma Programme at Newton College, who is looking to pursue a career in aerospace engineering in the future.

She started her MUN journey somewhat atypically, joining the club during the pandemic in 2021, and becoming an executive in April 2022 before having attended her first conference. After finally going to a committee, she fell even more in love with debating different perspectives, which motivated her to try her skills out in Harvard MUN 2023 – an unforgettable experience that prepared her to become the Under Secretary-General for 2023-2024.

As a violinist, Arantza spends her free time surrounded by music and making countless playlists for every genre in existence. She also enjoys swimming, reading, making origami cranes to hang from her ceiling, and sitting around to talk for hours on end.

Recognising MUN as a huge tool in developing her confidence and networking skills, she encourages every delegate to do their best and let go of their nerves on the speaking floor. Therefore, she wishes for all delegates to enjoy the exploration of these new perspectives, and to have an amazing Newton MUN 2024.

Director of ADHOC María Gracia Jimenez

María Gracia Jiménez is a second year IB student at Newton College. In the future, she looks to pursue a career within the fields of international relations and diplomacy.

Introduced to MUN in 2018, she quickly realized its significance beyond mere formal attire, witnessing delegates tackle global issues with eloquence and creativity. This inspired her to become an active participant, winning her first award that same year, and joining the secretariat in 2021.

In her free time, María Gracia deeply enjoys reading, baking and listening to music. At times, she also enjoys pretending she’s a poet and simply writes for hours. She considers there’s nothing better than a good coffee on a cold autumn afternoon with just the right amount of sunlight.

Joining MUN was one of the most impactful decisions she would make. MUN has not only equipped her with invaluable soft skills but also provided a sense of belonging and growth, akin to a second family: a place where she found a sense of belonging in that common interest of striving to make a difference; a place in which she grew and watched the next generations grow too. Despite career challenges, MUN remains a constant source of inspiration and belonging. As she concludes her MUN journey at Newton MUN 2024, she wishes all delegates good luck and, more than anything, to enjoy!

Director of UN women Anika Sahoo

Anika Sahoo is a second-year International Baccalaureate Diploma Programme student at Newton College. In the near future, she wishes to pursue a career in medical sciences.

In 2018, she joined the MUN club intrigued by the prospect of simulating the lives of diplomats, starting as an usher in Newton MUN 2018. Overcoming initial fears of public speaking, she gradually enhanced her soft skills, earning her first prize in 2019. Progressing within the club, she served as an Executive Leader in 2021 and as her delegation's Secretary-General in 2022. Throughout the 2023-2024 academic year, she continues to support and inspire new MUN enthusiasts at Newton College.

Beyond MUN, Anika enjoys playing the violin and piano, exploring biology concepts, and indulging in books. When she isn’t enjoying a quiet night alone with her headphones and books, she thoroughly enjoys irreplaceable moments with her friends.

Joining MUN has and will always remain one of the most grateful choices Anika opted for due to the experiences and skills she gained. It has not only helped her overcome fears but also sparked activism for women's rights, making her MUN journey enriching and rewarding.

Anika extends her best wishes to all Newton MUN 2024 delegates, aiming for a fulfilling MUN experience alongside her Secretariat colleagues.

Director of FUNSC Santiago Blanes

Santiago Blanes is a first-year student in the IB Diploma Programme at Newton College, highly motivated to pursue a career in Law in the future.

He started his MUN journey somewhat peculiarly compared to the others, joining the club in 2022 after the pandemic, and quickly becoming an executive in April 2023. He then continued with his MUN journey, attending more than 10 conferences both nationally and internationally. In turn this has motivated him to try his skills at Harvard MUN 2024, which is considered to be the biggest stage of them all; it was an unforgettable experience, one which could not be repeated anywhere else.

As a bit of an extrovert, Santiago spends most of his free time surrounded by friends and family spending every moment he can with them. He also enjoys listening to music, more specifically Rap or Hip Hop listening to artists such as Drake or J Cole.

Santiago considers that joining MUN will always be one of his best decisions he has ever made. It is a field where you can learn and grow, and even discover talents and gifts you never knew you had. Not only did Santiago come out of his comfort zone, he also learned about multiple new globally relevant issues, and has mainly enjoyed topics from the UNSC committee. MUN experiences have played an important role in forming him into the person he is today.

Director of crisis Marcelo Namisato

Marcelo Namisato is a first-year IB Diploma Programme student at Newton College, who wishes to pursue a medical career in the future. He has been a member of the Newton MUN Executive Committee since 2023.

He joined MUN in 2020 during the pandemic, being drawn to it due to its dynamic nature, and saw it as an opportunity to enhance his communication and networking skills. He started his MUN career with a virtual simulation hosted by Newton in 2021, where he was amazed by the active debates of the conference, regardless of it being online. Recently, he attended Harvard MUN 2024, where he gained invaluable experience navigating diverse perspectives and engaging in high-level debate. This exposure to an international stage broadened his understanding of global issues and refined his diplomatic skills.

Outside MUN, Marcelo is an avid basketball player, competing in various circuits and representing teams like Hopper and Leones. He also plays for the Newton Iguanas in tournaments such as ADCA and “La Liga de La Molina”. Additionally, he enjoys playing the guitar, inspired by his father.

Marcelo attributes much of his personal growth to his involvement in MUN, citing increased confidence, social skills, and global awareness. He encourages all Newton MUN 2024 delegates to embrace their MUN journey, learn from each other, and wishes them luck for the conference.

Director of SOCHUM Nina Porto

Nina Porto, a Newton College student, boasts an impressive track record in MUN and school activities. In her fourth year of conference participation and second year on the Newton MUN Executive Council, she also serves as a prefect and a member of Newton's GAIL delegation, advocating for global learning and sustainability. Additionally, Nina is actively involved in the NGO Oceanica, dedicating much of her time to community and environmental initiatives.

Despite initial challenges with virtual learning during the pandemic in 2020, Nina's brother Enzo encouraged her to persevere in MUN, a decision she never regretted. MUN taught Nina to take risks, address global issues responsibly, and maintain a balance between firmness in her beliefs and open-mindedness.

Outside MUN, Nina enjoys sharing music and spending time with friends, capturing memories with her digital camera. She finds inspiration from her home country, Brazil, particularly enjoying the beach and biking through the city.

Nina encourages all delegates, especially newcomers, to step out of their comfort zones at Newton MUN 2024. She pledges to work with the secretariat to create a welcoming learning environment for all delegates. Best of luck to everyone!

Director of UNESCO Isabella Carranza

Joining the MUN club in 2018 as an usher for Newton MUN, Isabella found a perfect outlet for expressing her ideas and developing skills. Her passion and determination grew with each conference, culminating in winning prizes and debating at Harvard MUN 2020 in Boston. Reflecting on her MUN experiences, she feels proud and grateful for the opportunities it provided.

In her leisure time, Isabella enjoys listening to music, watching rom coms, playing the violin, and spending time at the racecourse with her family.

Although Isabella accidentally stumbled upon the MUN club, she has grown immensely as a student and person, and is thankful to her mentors and teachers. She believes MUN offers valuable insights into society and the world, shaping individuals' lives. MUN clarified her future goals, instilling a desire to change the world.

Isabella advises Newton MUN 2024 delegates to speak up and give their best on every speech and every session, regardless of the adversities.
